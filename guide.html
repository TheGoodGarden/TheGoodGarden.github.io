Planting Guide


Vegetables


Vegetable Germination Time Period When to Plant (Australia) When to Pick Time to Grow Successive Planting
Tomato 5-10 days Spring or early summer When ripe and firm 60-85 days Yes, for 2-3 years
Lettuce 7-14 days Autumn or early spring When leaves reach desired size 30-60 days No, rotate with other crops yearly
Carrot 10-14 days Autumn or early spring When tops are 1 inch in diameter 70-80 days Yes, for 2-3 years
Spinach 7-14 days Autumn or early spring When leaves reach desired size 40-50 days No, rotate with other crops yearly
Broccoli 5-10 days Autumn or early spring When heads are tight and firm 50-70 days No, rotate with other crops yearly
Cucumber 7-10 days Spring or early summer When firm and deep green 50-70 days Yes, for 2-3 years
Pepper 7-14 days Spring or early summer When firm and fully colored 60-90 days Yes, for 2-3 years
Zucchini 7-10 days Spring or early summer When small to medium size, usually 6 inches length 40-60 days Yes, for 2-3 years
Beans 7-14 days Spring or early summer When pods are firm and crisp 50-60 days Yes, for 2-3 years
Beetroot 7-14 days Autumn or early spring When roots are 1-3 inches in diameter 50-70 days Yes, for 2-3 years
Capsicum 7-14 days Spring or early summer When firm and fully colored 60-80 days Yes, for 2-3 years
Pumpkins 7-14 days Spring or early summer When skin is hard and rind is tough 80-120 days No, rotate with other crops yearly
Rocket 7-14 days Autumn or early spring When leaves reach desired size 20-40 days No, rotate with other crops yearly
